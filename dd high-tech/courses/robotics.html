ROBOTICS AND AUTOMATION

Robotics refers to the field of engineering and science that deals with the design, development, and application of robots. Robots are mechanical or virtual devices that
are capable of performing tasks autonomously or semi-autonomously. They can be equipped with sensors, actuators, and control systems to perceive their environment,
make decisions, and carry out physical or cognitive tasks. Robotics involves various disciplines, including mechanical engineering, electrical engineering,
computer science, and artificial intelligence.

Automation, on the other hand, refers to the use of technology and machinery to perform tasks or processes with minimal or no human intervention. It involves the design and
implementation of systems that can operate automatically and efficiently. Automation can be applied to various industries and sectors, such as manufacturing, transportation,
agriculture, healthcare, and more. It aims to streamline operations, increase productivity, improve quality, and reduce human error. Automation can range from simple
repetitive tasks to complex and intelligent systems that mimic human behavior and decision-making processes.
